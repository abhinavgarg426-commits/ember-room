Chettinad Chicken Fry

Small pieces of chicken marinated overnight in our home-ground masala, then fried on the coal flame. Crispy outside, juicy inside. Takes 20 minutes because we don't rush it.

Meen Varuval (Seer Fish)

Coastal Tamil fish fry. Seer fish coated in chilli powder, turmeric, and curry leaves, shallow fried in coconut oil. We get fresh stock from Tuticorin every morning.

₹320

Paruppu Vada (3 pcs)

Golden fried dal vadas — the kind that are crispy on the outside and soft in the middle. Served with coconut chutney and sambaar. These are what regulars order first before looking at the menu.

₹90

Duck Kozhambu

Our family's showpiece. Duck slow-cooked in a rich tamarind and poppy seed gravy with a masala that's been in the family since 1962. Order ahead — we marinate for 24 hours.

₹420

Naatu Kozhi Kozhambu

Country chicken cooked with home-ground coconut, jeera, and whole spices. The chicken is from our supplier in Kanchipuram — what they call "real" chicken, not the farm-raised kind. Rich, tangy, and spicy.

Mutton biryani made the Chettinad way — dry, aromatic, with frimjee (whole spices) blooming in ghee. Served with dalcha and raita. We use Kaima rice from Salem, not the usual Basmati.

Our Story

Five Generations.
One Kitchen.

My great-grandmother, Valliammai, ran a small tiffin centre near the Chettinad railway station in the 1950s. Her specialty was Chettinad chicken — she used to say the secret was not the masala but the patience. She marinated overnight. She cooked on coal. She never compromised.

My grandmother, Dhanam, took the recipes to Chennai in 1978 and ran a mess for factory workers near Ambattur. My mother, Kavitha, expanded to a proper restaurant in 2002. I — Surya — took over in 2012 after a brief career in banking that taught me exactly how much I didn't want to be inside an office.

"The masala is ground fresh every morning at 5 AM. If it's not right, we don't serve it. Simple as that."

— Surya Kesavan, Third Generation
