Our Story

From My Grandmother's
Kitchen to Nungambakkam

My grandmother, Lakshmi Ammal, was a Chettiar housewife in Kanchipuram. Her cooking was famous in the neighbourhood — people would come to our house for weddings and poojas just for the food. In 1962, my grandfather Ramasamy decided she should open a restaurant. He rented a small space on Khader Nawaz Khan Road and put up 6 tables. She refused to change a single recipe.

She passed the kitchen to my mother, Radha, in 1990. My mother kept everything exactly the same — same masala proportions, same fish supplier, same rice from Thiruvannamalai. When I took over in 2010, I faced a choice: expand, add a 'modern' menu, open a second location. I didn't. I kept 6 tables. I kept the menu small. I kept the food exactly how my grandmother made it.

"We've turned down three offers to franchise. Chettinad food doesn't scale without changing. I won't change it."
